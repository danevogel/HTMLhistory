Evolution of HTML

  • 1991 - an open discussion group HTML-talk was started across the internet for the development of HTML.
  • 1992 - Dave Raggett from Hewlett-Packard's Labs in Bristol, England who was one of the enthusiastic of HTML, met Tim and upon returning England, Dave wrote a richer version of HTML called HTML+.
  • 1992 - NCSA showed interest in web and contributed to take the development of web a step further.
  • 1993 - Lou Montulli releases the Lynx browser version 2.0a.
  • 1993 - The first proposal for an HTML specification was an internet draft called "Hypertext Markup Language (HTML)" by Berners-Lee and Dan Connolly, was published.
  • 1994 - The World Wide Web Consortium forms.
  • 1995 - Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) completed the "HTML 2.0" specification.
  • 1997 - HTML 3.2 was published.
  • 1997 - HTML 4.0 in December of the same year.
  • 2008 - A working draft of HTML 5 is prepared.
